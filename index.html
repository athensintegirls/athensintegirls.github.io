Our Mission

As girls or non-binary individuals in math and STEM, all of us have seen the stark differences between the number of boys, girls, and non-binary participants in traditional math competitions or math-related activities in general. This monumental gap has discouraged many girls and non-binary people around the world from pursuing math, either as a hobby or a career. Through our activities, we hope to inspire a passion for this subject in more girls and non-binary individuals, helping to close this gap. Math is an amazing subject full of hidden puzzles and strategies, and together, we want to create an event full of joy where girls and non-binary participants can bond over the beauty of the subject.

FAQs

I’m interested but have no math competition experience. Can I still attend?

Of course, you should really attend this competition. In the past, many people have stated that INTEGIRLS gave them an entry into math competitions and they had no idea how it was until they attended the event. If you would like any pointers to understand how to prepare, you can feel free to email us.

I do not have an INTEGIRLS Chapter near my area, but I am interested in participating in the competition. Can I still attend?

We encourage students not only from Athens to attend, but also from other areas as well. You may reach out to us using the contact information at the bottom of this page.

Who can I contact if I have any questions?

You can contact us through email or Instagram at the links provided at the bottom of this page. If it is urgent, you can message us on Instagram or add “URGENT” in the subject line of your email.

I do not identify as female, but I would like to participate. How can I do that?

If you do not identify as female or non-binary, we encourage you to check out other opportunities we may share. You can contact athens@integirls.org to find more activities apt for you.

How can I contribute?

You may choose to volunteer for us by emailing us at athens@integirls.org or donate to support our non-profit initiatives. You can also email us to receive more information about how you can help.
